#### 2) Incentive gaming / “citation farming”
Any system that pays out on “being cited” can be gamed: contributors may produce low-quality or manipulative content designed to trigger model mentions, and brands may attempt to buy influence in ways that degrade trust and invite platform countermeasures (spam filters, source blacklists).

Voyage’s Data Layer proposes EEAT-based validation and community review, but implementing robust anti-sybil and quality control at scale is extremely hard, particularly if payouts are meaningful and participation is permissionless (risk remains).

#### 3) Coordination burden: protocol adoption
v402 depends on developers and content providers integrating SDKs/APIs and/or routing requests through a facilitator layer; protocol-layer startups often fail not due to tech quality but due to adoption inertia and chicken-and-egg dynamics (need content providers and index clients simultaneously).

Cloudflare’s “pay per crawl” highlights a competing approach with distribution leverage (already in the traffic path), which could compress the adoption window for independent protocols unless v402 offers unique features (attribution semantics, multi-chain settlement, creator payouts) that incumbents won’t ship (uncertain).

#### 4) Regulatory and reputational risk (crypto rails + “ads” implications)
Voyage’s messaging includes “brands can transparently incentivize mentions,” which can resemble paid placement/advertising; if that becomes opaque or misleading, it creates reputational risk and potential regulatory scrutiny depending on jurisdiction and disclosure requirements (especially in finance/health categories).
